GABBY GILBERT
Cursed to Find Myself is my own personal breakup memoir that discusses themes of love, betrayal, heartache, and finding one’s self after it’s all over. This story encapsulates my thoughts and feelings during key moments in the midst of and after my relationship with my first love, and how I managed to drag myself out of my depressive slump to find out who I truly was. The book is written to follow a playlist of songs by Olivia Rodrigo, with pages filled with her lyrics to the songs I felt best suited my story and ones that resonated the most with me.It can be read with or without listening to the songs attached to each chapter, but it is just a way to further include the readers into my thoughts and feelings throughout the story, and even now as I look back on everything. If anyone reading this is going through anything similar, just know that you are not alone. Thank you for taking the time to unpack and read my story.

HOLIDAY PRICE
Sensory Box. I aimed to delve into the realms of sensory perception, particularly emphasizing the tactile experience, and exploring repetition and patterns. My exploration revolved around discovering the solace, comfort, and sense of connection I find with interacting with my environment and trying to recreate that sense of fulfillment for those participating and interacting in my work. Some of the visual identity taken from personal sense of connection to water based fauna fostered by aquatic environments, feeling a sense of relatability due to these creatures also engaging with the world perceptually different than other people do at least tactilely. The outcome of all these explorations is a sensory box that you can enter and experience multiple tactile senses on the inside and outside of it. The goal is for the participants and observers to be able to interact with the piece, hopefully engaging in their own unique experience with the piece. The intention is to provide a closed space where the participant can have a moment of stillness, if they desire they can touch and interact with the structure, or they can just enjoy the visual experience.
JANGMI PAIK
Memories and Nostalgia. Ever since I moved to a different country, memories of Korea have become more special, forming the core of my identity. I believe stone lithography has a strong correlation to memory and pearls. Oysters build layers on top of an irritant, using it as a nucleus, much like people build distorted layers on top of the original memory that fades. Stone lithography involves a significant amount of irritation of the surface and layers of grease that form the image. The series of prints, using my childhood home and lithography as the core, build layers on top of each other, combining them into the theme of memories and nostalgia. I am also interested in the process of digital degradation. In a similar way to how digital copies are made and circulated, memories can be linked to copies of experiences stored in our minds. Each time we recall a memory, it may degrade and become distorted, influenced by factors such as personal biases, emotions, and subsequent experiences. Just as digital images lose quality with each copy, memories may lose fidelity and accuracy over time. This creates room for imagination, and I believe the distorted layers of memories are what make nostalgia special. I aimed to visualize this idea in a format of digital web: nostalgia.city.
