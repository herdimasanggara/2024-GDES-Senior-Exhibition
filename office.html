SUZIE HAN
For my project,Emotional Mindspaces, I aim for readers to navigate a journey through the mindset we have during emotional stress or heightened emotion. In my first book, I include images and writing that reflect my perspective when grappling with depression. It feels as if I am in a dark pit and everything around me is gray. In my second book, I talk about my experience with anxiety and how it restricts my ability to act freely. Each book is printed on cardstock with clear acrylic covers and bound with a Coptic stitch. The covers are clear because they symbolize my feelings in a glass box. With this project, I strive for transparency about my feelings and as much as I want to hide them, they are on display for all to see.
WENDY NGUYEN
Bringing It Home. It was a lovely coincidence that my brother just so happened to be getting married in Vietnam at the same time I was conjuring up how I was going to go about what I wanted to do. I took the opportunity with the two weeks I had earlier in the year to observe and capture everything I possibly could that could fuel my inspiration while in Vietnam: textures, building structures, street signs, the flowers and plants; anything I could lay my eyes on, I wanted to capture. With all of this, I took all of my observations and began a rebrand campaign for my mom’s small business nail salon by bringing Vietnamese elements and aesthetics into modern brand design including a new logo, business cards, service menus, and a revival of a vintage form of advertisement in a paper fan. I am bringing these Vietnamese elements to my home in the United States by means of the design branding, and I am simultaneously bringing bits and pieces of my mom’s home here into the salon. This project exists a “thank you” to her.
STELLA TESSAROLLO
The Art of Climate Education aims to use art as a catalyst for change. This book is a collection of primarily installation based artworks that seek to educate viewers on climate issues and inspire them to take action. Each piece of art was selected based on its ability to convey an important message about the need to conserve our environment. Some works use appreciation as the motivating factor, while others propose solutions to how we might tangibly make a difference in protecting our world. The goal is to invite curiosity and inspire viewers to learn in a new way. During my time in GDES, I discovered my passion for designing informational publications for climate education. This project is a culmination of my love for art, research, bookbinding, and the desire to make a small impact on the world by inspiring someone to make a positive change in their lifestyle to help protect our Earth.

The website contains educational content, such as an “Introduction to alt text” section that defines both alt text and image description, and shows examples of what these might look like. WILLIAM HERTZLER
Doodle Anthology sets out to compile and remaster doodles I created on schoolwork throughout my education from kindergarten to senior year of high school. The anthology aims to provide insight into the evolution of my artistic style, creative expression, and interests over the years with additional information about their subject matter, context and their significance to me. Old schoolwork is not a resource many people utilize or still have access to. Many of the homework sheets that the images were sourced from were found loose and faded in a dusty box under my bed. The doodles have been scanned, isolated, and retraced in a digital format, providing clarity to long forgotten scribbles on crumpled sheets of paper. Doodling as a practice is often associated with procrastination, so I wanted to explore the idea of creating artwork out of a product that came from a desire not to work. I set out to create a repository for an art form that is normally discarded and explore the biases that affect what kinds of art we decide to preserve.

YUYING SHI
This project creates a 2024 calendar focused on the Chinese 24 Solar Terms. Each page represents a specific solar term, showcasing associated traditional foods and cultural significance through uniquely designed icons and color schemes. This calendar is not only a record of time but also a modern interpretation and visual presentation of traditional Chinese solar term culture. Printed in color, each icon is rich in detail and vibrant in hues, designed to engage users in exploring the culinary customs and cultural depth behind each solar term. The calendar serves both practical daily use and as an educational and cultural dissemination tool, enhancing understanding and interest in traditional solar term knowledge.

ANDERS JENSEN
slowing… slowing… slowing… slowing is the hand; slowing is transparency; slowing is personal; slowing is caring; slowing is natural; slowing is generative; slowing is a reduction; slowing is human. The infinite and exponential growth proposed by capitalist systems inherently prioritizes speed and mass production, eliminating human aspects within the labor structure. In looking at the individual as a part of the whole, the larger structure can be looked at as a machine, minimizing individual impact and sense of self. In my own angst about my place within society as a designer, visualizing a means to work against the system manifests itself in material and processes. Inducing decay onto the material itself, is an act of bringing the steel back towards nature, fighting from the embodiment of massive, monolithic systems in place. A companion website located at anders-makes.com/slowing presents a form of ‘rust’, induced by user interaction, developing over time in a digital environment. The web space acts as its own container for digital processes. In an exercise of material and process, this project is an exploration that is not meant to provide answers, but rather introduce questions and promote dialogue for alternative existences.
