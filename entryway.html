AARON THOMPSON
Shoulder to Shoulder is an impactful sculptural installation that deeply probes the essence of solidarity, self-sacrifice, and oppression within the context of protest. It profoundly reflects the collective spirit of resistance and the incredible power of unity against systemic injustices. Central to this installation is the use of burlap and mannequins, each chosen for their symbolic resonance and integral role in conveying the themes of human resilience, the flammability of change, and the faceless universality of the struggle for rights and recognition. Burlap, with its coarse texture and simple, unassuming presence, is the primary material in this sculpture, draped over mannequins to represent the masses standing in solidarity. This choice of fabric is pivotal, not only for its aesthetic and tactile qualities but for its symbolic weight. Burlap embodies the rugged endurance of the collective will, signifying the grassroots nature of social movements and the interconnectedness of individuals fighting for a common cause. It speaks to the strength found in unity and the rough-hewn, diverse fabric of society coming together to face systemic challenges.

With so many passions available, it is difficult to fit them all into one career when joining the workforce. Expanding our mindset beyond a singular job title is not only beneficial but necessary as AI begins to reduce the value of formal hard skills. Think beyond a career and focus on the core aspects that we enjoy when working. The BE PASSIONATE campaign advocates for this mindset shift by helping young individuals learn how to input their soft skills and trait adjectives into their resume. JASMINE KNIGHT
Cocoa & Berry: An Exploration of Brand Design is a project I have wanted to do for a long time; it combines all the things I love about graphic design! Cocoa & Berry came to life via my love for hot chocolate. I wanted to create a brand that I would seriously buy from. The brand is a mix of bright, cute, elegant, and simple. I chose to host the majority of the brand content on a website. On this site alongside the beverage itself, there is some other merchandise that has been designed such as totes, stickers, and pins. The brand itself was designed after various aspects of a hot chocolate and berry fusion. The main logo is decorative and includes elements that mimic things such as the swirl of whipped cream or a cherry that tops off the warm drink. This is a recurring theme throughout the brand.
COLBY CLARKE
Swapparel My pieces satirize how graphic designers don't like when people change existing structures, in this case apparel, that have been already perfected, according to them. I decided to turn apparel design on its head by taking the essence of popular articles of apparel and swapping them with another one to create new mashed-up clothing, or "swapparel."The designs themselves are poking fun at the tiny white behind-the-neck tags filled with an overload of information which are so much of a nuisance that they are often cut out. To challenge that, I made them the center of attention so that there is no way to ignore or remove them. The Hoodie Hat makes fun of the materials list and the odd things they are forced to disclose about what the clothing is made of. The Mug Hoodie mocks the laundry list of required steps and tips for cleaning your clothes and their accompanying cryptic symbols. The Shirt Pants tease the unregulated sizing charts and how they can often be unreliable.
